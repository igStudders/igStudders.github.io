    Edit title
    List your work experience, add different positions you held and describe your responsibilites. Don't forget to highlight your biggest achievements! See tips & examplesSee less
    Avoid long descriptions and text blocks. Use bullets to separate text.

    Carefully read the job description, and match your experience to the employer's requirements. Delete any unnecessary information. Your job description should not exceed 8-10 points. Do not describe your old positions in detail.

    When you describe your professional achievements, use the formula: I solved problem X and Y resulting in Z.

    Present results in the form of numbers, percentages eg., I increased sales by 20% within 12 months. Avoid giving specific monetary values in relation to projects. Such information can be sensitive.

    Edit title
    Fill in only the information you would like to show on your resume. Leave other fields empty. See tips & examplesSee less
    Your should take your picture against a light background. You should be wearing business attire. A small smile is acceptable. Do not add informal photos.

    Remember that a photo on a resume is not standard in some countries. If you are applying for a job in the US or the UK, do not add photos.

    It is important to include a current phone number and e-mail address. Do not fill the fields related to your place of residence if the employer does not require it.

    Here you will find additional information, i.e., your place of birth, sex, marital status, nationality, and country. You do not have to fill those fields if you don't want to.

    Add the name of the position for which you are applying. The text will appear under your name, creating a personal touch and showing that you tailored your resume to that position.

    You can add your website, as well as your social media handles. Hint: When you add your LinkedIn profile, remember to personalize your URL first.

    Summary (Optional)

    • Start with your professional title

      Starting a resume summary with your professional title informs a recruiter that your resume is relevant e.g., Marketing Manager with 5+ years of experience.

    • Add two or three achievements

      Add numbers and details. Focusing on results allows you to stand out and proves you’re the right candidate eg., Increased sales by 36% over 6 months.

    • Tailor it to the job offer

      Find keywords from the job description, including skills and adjectives (dedicated, hardworking) and add them to your resume summary.

    Edit title
    Add different schools, colleges, and universities that you attended. Describe them by adding information about your majors or extracurricular activities. See tips & examplesSee less
    If your work experience is longer than two years, the education section should be included on the resume following your experience section.

    Add your field of study, the name of your university, and the degree you obtained. Do not include information about your high school or technical school (unless the school's profile is strongly related to your profession).

    You can add academic achievements if you want. These might include an above-average academic record, scholarships, awards, etc...

    Edit title
    Show the recruiter what you can do. Add different skills such as "Problem solving" or "Microsoft Word" to showcase your abilities. See tips & examplesSee less
    Place your skills on the first page of your resume. Use templates that show them in the side column.

    Match skills to the job, and place the most important skills on the top of the list.

    Use numbers and facts, for example: Manage sales team of 30+ people. Think about how you can stand out. Instead of writing knowledge of MS Excel, put advanced knowledge of MS Excel (macros, pivot tables).

    Describe a level of your skill or use a rating.
